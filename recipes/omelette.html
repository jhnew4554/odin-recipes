Omelette

Description

In cuisine, an omelette (also spelled omelet) is a dish made from beaten eggs, fried with butter or oil in a frying pan (without stirring as in scrambled egg). It is quite common for the omelette to be folded around fillings such as chives, vegetables, mushrooms, meat (often ham or bacon), cheese, onions or some combination of the above. Whole eggs or egg whites are often beaten with a small amount of milk, cream, or water.

Ingredients

  • eggs
  • salt
  • butter
  • cheese

Steps

  1. Beat eggs-in a small bowl,combine 2 eggs and a pinch of salt then beat with a fork until frothy
  2. Heat skillet- place a small non-stick pan over a medium heat and melt in 1/2 Tbsp of butter.Once butter is melted and bubbly,add beaten eggs and reduce heat to low
  3. Pull cooked eggs with a spatula into the center, allowing the liquid eggs to fill in the place
  4. Flip once eggs are neatly set,the omelette slides easily on the skillet and you can get a spatula underneath, flip it over.Turn off the heat
  5. Fold-add cheese and your favorite toppings,fold the omelette and slide it onto a plate to serve
